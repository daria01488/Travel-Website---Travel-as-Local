Hamburg, Berlin, Prague & Munich: Urban Charm with a Local Twist

Urban vibes, riverside sunsets, and ice cream that melts too fast – this trip is summer at full volume.

This trip is perfect if you love:

Urban vibes, riverside sunsets & historic charm

Hit the heart of Europe’s coolest cities in one unforgettable route. From Hamburg’s buzzing harbor to Berlin’s street-art alleyways, Prague’s fairytale skyline to Munich’s sunlit beer gardens—this trip delivers big city thrills mixed with local delights.

Hamburg

Hamburg’s waterfront is alive with maritime energy—explore the fish market at dawn, wander the red-brick Speicherstadt, and soak up modern vibes at the Elbphilharmonie plaza.

In the evening, head to St. Pauli for live music and harbor-side bars, then catch a sunset boat ride on the Alster lakes. Hamburg proves that port cities can be effortlessly cool.

Berlin

Berlin never sleeps—hit its world-famous clubs, browse constantly-evolving street art in Kreuzberg, and discover tucked-away cafés in Prenzlauer Berg.

Don’t miss the East Side Gallery murals, the tranquility of Tiergarten, and currywurst stands on every corner. Berlin’s blend of history and nightlife makes it a must-visit.

Prague

Prague feels straight out of a storybook—wander the Charles Bridge at dawn, admire Gothic spires in Old Town, and sip honey mead in cozy taverns.

Climb to Prague Castle for panoramic views, lose yourself in narrow cobblestone alleys, and watch street musicians under lantern-lit arches. Prague’s magic is timeless.

Munich

Munich blends tradition with modern comfort—relax in sprawling beer gardens, admire Baroque architecture in Marienplatz, and explore museums along the Kunstareal.

For a green escape, stroll the English Garden or picnic by the Isar River. And if your timing’s right, join the festive spirit of an outdoor beer hall under the summer sky.

How to connect these cities?

• Hamburg → Berlin: Regional trains depart hourly. The journey takes about 2 hours and costs around €35–€45. For a budget-friendly alternative, FlixBus runs several daily departures in about 3 hours from €14.99—perfect if you don’t mind a slightly longer ride in comfortable coach seating.
• Berlin → Prague: EuroCity trains run several times daily. Expect a 4½ hour ride for about €50. Alternatively, FlixBus offers direct coaches in around 4–5 hours from €17–€25, with onboard Wi-Fi and power outlets—ideal for working or relaxing on the way.
• Prague → Munich: Direct trains take roughly 6 hours and cost €45–€55. For a cheaper overnight option, FlixBus runs the route from €20 and lets you sleep en route, arriving refreshed in the morning.
