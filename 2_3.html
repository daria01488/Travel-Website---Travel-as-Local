Italia from North to South: Milan to Palermo

A long, loving journey through the heart of Italy – golden leaves, rich coffee, ancient stones, and stories at every stop.

Northern Italy Highlights

Milan – Venice – Bologna – Florence

Dive into Italy’s artistic and culinary heartlands on this enriching route. Start amid Milan’s fashion streets, drift along Venice’s canals, savor Bologna’s legendary cuisine, and discover Florence’s Renaissance wonders—perfect for foodies and culture seekers alike.

Milan

Italy’s style capital pulses with energy—visit the Duomo’s rooftop terraces, browse designer boutiques in the Quadrilatero, and unwind during aperitivo hour in hidden side-street bars.

Don’t miss Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II’s elegant arcades or a stroll through the Brera district, where art studios and cozy cafés set the scene.

Venice

Glide under historic bridges on a gondola, explore labyrinthine calli and campos, and sample cicchetti in family-run bàcari where locals linger over wine.

Sunrise on the Rialto Bridge and sunset from the Zattere waterfront reveal the city’s timeless beauty and quiet magic.

Bologna

Known as “la grassa” (the fat one), Bologna entices with fresh tagliatelle al ragù and tortellini in brodo. Wander its endless porticoes and climb the Asinelli Tower for rooftop views.

Explore medieval squares, sip espresso in student-filled cafés, and feel the city’s warm, welcoming spirit.

Florence

The cradle of the Renaissance dazzles with the Duomo’s dome, Botticelli’s “Birth of Venus” at the Uffizi, and sunset al fresco on the Ponte Vecchio.

Lose yourself among leather workshops, artisan gelaterias, and olive-oil-lit trattorias where every meal feels like a celebration.

Southern Italy Sojourn

Rome – Naples – Palermo

Journey into Italy’s sun-kissed south: wander Rome’s ancient streets, savor Naples’ authentic pizza by the bay, and lose yourself in Palermo’s vibrant markets and sunlit mosaics. This route blends history, flavor, and Mediterranean warmth.

Rome

Wander through layers of history—from the Colosseum’s grandeur to hidden temples in Trastevere. Rome’s lively piazzas and baroque fountains invite you to linger with a gelato in hand.

Don’t miss sunrise at the Forum, an espresso by the Pantheon, and evening strolls along the Tiber under softly lit bridges.

Naples

Known as pizza’s birthplace, Naples thrills with Neapolitan pies fresh from wood-fired ovens. Explore underground catacombs, colorful street art, and grand vistas of Vesuvius over the bay.

Sample sfogliatelle at local pasticcerie and watch the sunset over Castel dell’Ovo for true Neapolitan magic.

Palermo

Palermo pulses with Sicilian soul: lively markets brim with citrus, seafood, and spices, while Norman palaces and Arab mosaics tell tales of a rich past.

Meander through the winding Kalsa quarter, sip coffee under orange trees, and end your days with street food under a Mediterranean sky.

• Bologna → Florence: Fast regional trains connect in ~40 min for about €10. FlixBus routes take ~1 h from €5 and run throughout the day, a great way to save a few euros.
• Rome → Naples: Frecciarossa & Italo trains run hourly. Journey time ~1 h 10 min, fares from €25–€45. For a cheaper slower ride, FlixBus coaches take ~2 h 30 min from €12 with Wi-Fi and reclining seats.
• Naples → Palermo: Overnight Intercity Notte trains depart nightly. Travel time ~10–11 h, tickets from €45. Budget-minded? FlixBus runs a ~12 h coach from €20, so you can sleep en route and arrive refreshed.
